Why only compare Living Area and Year Built?

When I started this project, my instinct was to compare homes on as many features as possible — bedrooms, land size, zoning, sale history. But the Lexington assessor's database publishes 35+ fields per property, and most of them either don't vary meaningfully across homes or duplicate information in other fields.

So I computed a correlation heatmap across every numeric feature against the property's 2024 Assessment. Three features stood out:

  • Living Area — the strongest single predictor
  • Building Percent Good — how depreciated the home is (a proxy for age + condition)
  • Year Built — correlated with both design era and neighborhood development patterns

That's why this tool defines "similar homes" as ones built within 7 years of yours and within 300 sq ft of your living area. Other features either weren't predictive enough to justify narrowing the comparison pool, or weren't captured in the town's public data at all (floor plan quality, kitchen condition, energy efficiency, proximity to schools).
