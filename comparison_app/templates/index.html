    About this project

    Why I Built This

    I live in Lexington, MA. My family noticed that our property taxes increased significantly from 2023 to 2024, even though we made no changes to our house. I wanted to determine if this was part of a town-wide trend or a unique increase in the town's assessment of our property. If it was the latter, this data could help us file an abatement and possibly reduce our taxes.

    With the help of my analysis, I unfortunately concluded that it was indeed a proportional town-wide increase, so our taxes would have to stay :(

    I extended this tool to help any Lexington resident.

    What I Learned
    • Web scraping the town's database, collecting 35+ features per property.
    • Multithreading to cut scraping time from 2 hours to under 10 minutes, with a lock to keep the shared DataFrame thread-safe.
    • Using a correlation heatmap to isolate the features most predictive of assessment value, which guided which properties to treat as "similar."
    How the Analysis Works

    For any Lexington address, the tool finds homes built within seven years of yours and within 300 sq ft of your living area, then ranks your 2024 assessment against that peer group. "Recent sales" are filtered to 2022–2024 so the comparison reflects current market values, not pre-pandemic pricing.

    The features most correlated with assessment value are Living Area, Building Percent Good, and Year Built — so those are what the comparison uses. Other factors (floor plan, kitchen quality, energy efficiency, proximity to the town center) aren't captured in the assessor's database.
